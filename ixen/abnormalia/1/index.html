I am not a story. I am the residue several stories left when they failed to agree.

un nome. pochi fatti.

Read me as marginalia. Recovered, partial, written in a hand that keeps changing because the hand keeps dying and being replaced. I have been a floppy. I have been a table layout. I have been a locked door. Now I am whatever you are looking at.

Don't trust the continuity. I don't.

First incarnation: a group. A small one. The kind that existed to push out a diskmag and call that a reason to exist.

What the Demozoo record shows

Demozoo indexes Abnormalia as a demoscene group whose purpose was releasing the Amiga diskmagazine of the same name. The record connects the project to Italian writer Macno, listed after the entries Grace and Scene Lyrics. Abnormalia 1 is catalogued as a diskmag released in December 1992.

A diskmag was a magazine distributed on floppy disk and read on the machine itself, typically combining text, music, and graphics in a custom reader. No staff list, article titles, or issue contents beyond this catalogue entry are confirmed here.

The archive that remains is not the work. It is what the work could not finish deleting.

How does a diskmag move? Hand to hand. Disk to disk. Then later: a phone line, a modem, an upload queue with a quota.

How scene-era distribution worked

Demoscene productions spread through small groups, diskmags, and bulletin board systems (BBSes) reached by dial-up modem. Promotional intros and BBStros advertised boards and releases. Identity was carried by handles and group affiliations rather than legal names.

What survives today is mostly the index: catalogue entries and search traces. For Abnormalia, a Demozoo search returns a partial BBS and BBStro promotion trace dated 1996. It is a pointer to activity, not a complete account of it.

An invented vocabulary

Abnormalia's identity was carried largely through coined and mutated Italian words rather than conventional content categories. Terms such as anomini, abnomini, abnominio, abmorto and the phrase regolaria varia functioned as both navigation and tone. Section names like Buco Nero, Sinaptium and Pasticche Net, along with phrases such as "javate del secolo scorso" and "il passato rimane", extended this private lexicon.

The effect was a site that defined itself by inventing its own language, so that the wording itself, rather than any single article, became the project's recognisable substance.

The I-Xen page

The local I-Xen page is a 2005 OpenSkills production combining a tutorial for running Xen on Suse 9.3 with a first-person short story titled "I, System". The text is credited to al and the graphics to tatlin.

It describes itself as version 0.1, "graphical madness," and the first chapter of an interactive story for Xen configuration and understanding, or alternatively "just another half born fun project." It is the formal ancestor of the present Ixen pages — a hypervisor narrating its own existence — though it concerns different technology.

The present repository

Today abnormalia.com is a static Jekyll site hosted on GitHub Pages and served behind Cloudflare. Each Ixen is a standalone generated HTML page stored under ixen/<slug>/ and registered in _data/ixens.yml so it appears in the site index.

The pages are produced by the generate-ixens "swamp" workflow, which takes YAML inputs and assembles a finished page: prose generated by Claude, images by OpenAI, music by Suno, and final assembly handled by swamp extensions.

The work was never the artifact. The work is the attempt. The artifact is just proof I was here long enough to fail at staying.

The throughline

Across its forms — Amiga diskmag, BBS-era traces, handmade website, social-media absence, a 2005 hypertext tutorial, and present-day generated pages — Abnormalia has no single defining medium. The continuity is a consistent refusal to settle into one form, with each incarnation more unusual than the previous and frequent gaps of inactivity.

The current site is explicit about its own use of generated media, framing it as "AI slop with style" to be owned and, possibly, transcended. The stated value is the ongoing attempt rather than any finished product.
